Bio

I was born in Ukraine in 1994, in the small town of Kamianske. Since childhood, I have been drawn to photography, perhaps it was because my grandfather was also into photography and at home he had an old film camera and a printing laboratory.

So my journey into photography began as a childhood dream, but it took a profound turn when I experienced an episode teetering on the edge of life and death. This pivotal moment prompted me to realize my passion for creating art, recognizing that I have only one life to fulfill this calling.

I am a dreamer and a romantic, and I like to convey the feeling of unreality in my photography; the fragile feeling of magic in the frame is important to me. I like it to feel like a dream.

My photography is about life in imaginary worlds. Angels and mermaids exist there, whatever you want can be there. I am fascinated by the connection between nature and people, and I feel a fragile calm in this connection.

Mermaids Project captures the ethereal beauty of mysterious underwater worlds, bringing to life the mesmerizing world of mythical sea creatures. Invites viewers on a visual journey, exploring the boundaries between dreams and reality.

And this year in winter, when I was haunted by dark emotions, I found in them inspiration for a new project, Flora incognita.
